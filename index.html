The JJ Smith Yobody marketing engine

This document shows every traffic source we have, which funnel each one goes to, what we sell them, and the order we turn it all on.

Five traffic sources. Two funnels. Two offers. One product underneath. The split is simple: traffic that already knows JJ goes straight to a sales page, because they only need one page to decide. Traffic that does not know JJ goes to the quiz, because an offer put in front of a stranger gets ignored. Anyone who does not buy becomes a lead we own, and we sell them again on the next challenge.

The pieces underneath

The two routes, the two offers, all five traffic sources, the order we turn them on, and the one thing that blocks the whole engine.
The two funnels, side by side
Funnel 1
Warm traffic: sell on the page
These people already know JJ. They read her books, follow her, or just bought her supplement. They do not need a funnel to warm up, but they do still need convincing. It just all happens on one page, using Problem, Agitate, Solve: name the problem, make it real, show the fix, then make the offer.
  • Sources: supplement buyers, email list, JJ social
  • Goes to: a sales page (ongoing or challenge angle)
  • Offer: $47/mo, framed as $1.57 a day
  • Why it converts: they trust her, the price is low, the guarantee removes the risk
  • Its job: revenue now, and proof the offer works
Funnel 2
Cold traffic: diagnose, then sell
These people have never heard of JJ and were not looking for a coach. They were scrolling. Put an offer in front of them and they ignore it. So we diagnose them first with the quiz, give them a real answer, and take the email. Even if they never buy, we now own the lead.
  • Sources: paid ads and AI clone videos, each built around one pain point
  • Goes to: the quiz
  • Offer: only after the diagnosis, never before
  • Why it works: they feel understood before anything is sold
  • Its job: build the list, then convert it over time
The two offers

Same product underneath. Same daily price to walk in. What changes is the reason to act now, and what she can pay.

Offer 1
The monthly: the easiest yes
Priced so low it stops being a decision. $1.57 a day, cancel anytime, 14-day money back. There is almost nothing left to object to, which is exactly the point. This is the default we push on warm traffic and the one the quiz lands on.
  • Price: $47/mo, framed as $1.57 a day
  • Risk: 14-day money back, cancel anytime
  • Urgency: none, and it does not need any
  • Wins on: conversion rate
Offer 2
The challenge: the best LTV
She can still walk in at $1.57 a day, so we lose none of the low friction. But the challenge adds a real deadline, a shared group, and a pay in full option for the whole 12 weeks. Commitment goes up, cash comes up front, and she stays longer.
  • Price: same monthly, or pay in full and save
  • Urgency: real, doors close, limited spots
  • Seasonal: Mother’s Day, Sexy by Christmas, New Year, summer
  • Wins on: lifetime value, and it is what we sell the list

The order we turn it on

The engine only works in this order. Each stage pays for the next, and each gate has to be true before we spend on the one after it. This is how we make it hard to fail, instead of betting everything on ad spend up front.

01
Prove the core on free warm traffic
Supplements, email, JJ social. Zero ad spend. The gate: does the monthly page convert her own audience, and do they stay past month one? If JJ's own fans do not buy, no amount of ad money fixes it. We already learned that the hard way.
02
Turn the supplement flow on
500 bottles a day is a warm audience we are currently not monetizing. A QR on the bottle and a post-purchase page costs nothing. The gate: a working conversion rate from buyer to subscriber.
03
Get the number that unlocks ads
Conversion rate plus churn gives us what a customer is worth over her life. The gate: a real LTV. Without it, ads are gambling. With it, ads are arithmetic, and we know exactly what we can pay for a cold woman.
04
Open the cold engine
Paid ads and AI clone content into the quiz. The gate: cost per lead and cost per customer must come in under the LTV we just proved. If it does, this scales as far as we want to spend.
05
Monetize the list on repeat
Every non-buyer is already ours. Three or four dated challenge launches a year, sold to a list we did not pay for twice. The gate: revenue per launch per thousand leads.
06
Re-run it for the next coach
Nothing above is JJ specific except the face and the voice. Once it works, Mike Rashid is a swap, not a rebuild. That is the actual business.
One thing blocks the whole engine
Every page promises "1-on-1 calls with JJ" and "a text from JJ every morning." A woman reads that and believes JJ Smith personally calls her. We need JJ, Meka, and legal to land the exact framing before we point paid traffic, her audience, and her name at it. The honest version is also the stronger one: for 15 years JJ could only reach women through a book that hands over a plan and then disappears. Now she can actually be there, every morning, for every woman. No competitor can say that. This gets solved before we scale, not during.

The quiz funnel, step by step
1

The traffic: one ad per pain point

JJ's own content and paid Meta ads bring in women 40+. Each post or ad leads with a different pain ("why losing weight after 40 feels impossible," "you've tried everything," "the weight at your middle," "coming off the shot"). Each angle opens a landing page that matches that exact message, so the promise she clicked is the promise she lands on.
Measure spend, reach, and click rate per angle. Which pains bring the cheapest, best traffic.
2

The quiz page headline matches the ad

She clicked an ad about one specific problem, so the quiz page headline says that same problem back to her. If the ad was about the weight sitting at her middle, the page opens on that. The headline changes for every pain point, but the quiz underneath is always the same one, so we get dozens of tailored entry points at the build cost of one.
Measure how many who land actually start the quiz, per pain point. Scale the ones that pull people in.
3

She answers, and the result is held back

A one-minute quiz. She answers and feels understood along the way. At the end she is shown her own words back with a curiosity gap, but not the answer yet. No pattern name here. That is what makes her want to unlock the full breakdown.
Measure completion, which questions lose people, and which patterns our traffic skews to.
◆

The email gate: she becomes a lead we own

To unlock her full result she gives her email. At that moment we have her whole picture: name, email, her pain, her pattern, what she tried, her goal. Every woman who reaches here is a known, tagged lead, whether she buys or not. That is the asset. It is what makes the bring-back loop possible.
Measure how many reach the gate and how many give email. This is our lead count.
4

The full diagnosis, where the real selling happens

Once she is in, she gets the real thing: a diagnosis composed from her own answers that names her pattern (one of nine) and explains why she has been stuck, then shows how JJ fixes her specific pattern. It is delivered in full before any offer. The diagnosis does the selling, because it is true and it lifts the blame off her. No hard pitch.
Measure how many read the diagnosis through to the offer, by pattern.
5

Optional: the JJ voice demo

Before the offer, she can have a short call with JJ. The quiz already gave us her answers, so JJ opens the call knowing her situation, which keeps it short and makes it feel personal. This is optional and we test it. It either lifts the buy rate enough to justify the cost per call, or we turn it off. We do not assume it helps.
Measure buy rate with the demo on versus off, and the cost per call.
6

The offer & checkout

The diagnosis did the selling, so the offer only has to make the decision easy and safe. She sees the same offer as the warm traffic: the monthly at $47, framed as $1.57 a day, cancel anytime, 14-day money back. If a challenge is running, she can take that instead and pay in full. (Prices pending TJ.)
Measure purchase rate, sliced by angle and by pattern; entry-price vs. pay-in-full; checkout drop-off.
↷

Bring-back loop: the leads who did not buy

Because we own each woman's full picture, the ones who did not buy get email matched to her pattern, not one generic blast. The emotional eater gets one path, the midlife woman another, re-approaching the offer over time at near-zero cost. This is why we count revenue per lead, not just per buyer. Buyers, meanwhile, become the proof that feeds new content at the top.
Measure re-conversion over time by segment; revenue per lead; testimonials that feed the top.
Two things running underneath
Measurement & testing
The rail down the side reads every stage: where people drop off, sliced by angle and by pattern. And the big choices (like whether the voice demo is worth its cost) are run as A/B tests, not guesses. The funnel improves itself over time.
The same machine, per coach
Nothing here is JJ-only in its shape. For a new coach we swap the angles, the quiz's read, the voice, and the offer, and the whole funnel runs again. Mike Rashid is next.

This is the operating picture, not the build status. Where each part stands (done / in progress / not started) can be marked on top when we walk through it together. Detailed specs, copy, and the live build sit behind each part.
